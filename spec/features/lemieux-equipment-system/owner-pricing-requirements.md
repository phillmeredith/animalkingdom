# Owner pricing requirements — LeMieux Equipment System

**Confirmed by [OWNER] 2026-03-28**

## Rarity tier
All LeMieux items are **Epic** or **Legendary** rarity only. No Common, Uncommon, or Rare LeMieux items exist in this system.

## Pricing philosophy
Harry must save up a significant number of coins to afford these items. They are premium aspirational purchases, not casual buys.

## Suggested price bands (UX spec must define exact values)
- **Epic** items — mid-range LeMieux products (boots, fly hoods, headcollars, stable items): suggest 300–600 coins
- **Legendary** items — premium/flagship LeMieux products (rugs, saddle pads, saddlery): suggest 700–1200 coins

These ranges are starting points. The UX spec must define exact prices per category that feel achievable through sustained play but require real saving effort.

## Rarity badge treatment
LeMieux items display the existing **Epic** (`var(--purple)` tint pair) and **Legendary** (`var(--amber)` tint pair) rarity badge treatment — same as animal cards. No new rarity colours are introduced.

## Design intent
The high price point is intentional. It creates:
- A meaningful savings goal for Harry
- Pride of ownership when an item is finally purchased
- Clear visual hierarchy between LeMieux items and cheaper in-game consumables
- A reason to play arcade games, race, and sell animals — the economy loop feeds into equipment purchases

## UX spec must address
1. How does the shop communicate "you need X more coins" to Harry when he can't afford an item yet?
2. Is there a wishlist or "save for this" mechanic, or just the price displayed?
3. How do Epic vs Legendary items visually differ in the shop grid beyond the badge?
4. Does the equip screen show locked slots for items Harry hasn't bought yet?
