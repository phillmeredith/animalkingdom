## Notes on selection rationale

### Curriculum coverage
The 200 cards collectively cover:
- All 7 continents (UK/Europe, Africa, Asia, North America, South America, Oceania, Marine/Polar)
- 11 biomes (urban, temperate_forest, tropical_rainforest, grassland_savanna, desert, arctic_polar, aquatic_marine, mountain_alpine, freshwater, prehistoric, coral_reef)
- All UK National Curriculum geography regions studied at KS1–KS2
- Migration (Humpback Whale, Monarch region, Arctic Fox, Wildebeest context, Emperor Penguin, Sea Turtle, Flamingo, Canada Goose)
- Conservation statuses across the full IUCN spectrum: LC through EX
- All habitat builder roles: producer, herbivore, carnivore, omnivore, apex predator, decomposer

### Conservation messaging
The collection deliberately includes:
- 8 Critically Endangered species (to make CR meaningful, not routine)
- 12 Endangered species
- 10 Vulnerable species
- 5 Near Threatened species
- Historical extinctions (Dodo, Quagga) to contextualise EX status
- Conservation success stories (Red Kite, White-tailed Eagle, Bald Eagle, American Alligator)

### Inclusion audit
- Gender of animals: vocabulary cards include both male and female terms (ewe/ram, doe/buck, drake/duck)
- Representation: no continent is a single biome; Africa includes forest, desert, wetland, savanna
- Invisible animals: included lesser-known but educationally rich species (Gharial, Shoebill, Mantis Shrimp, Axolotl) alongside iconic species
- Age appropriateness: all facts verified as suitable for 8–10 year olds; no graphic predation statistics

### Note on card count
The catalogue above runs to 260 numbered entries because some category reorganisations occurred during drafting (notably the Wild section was organised by region within one category). The JSON file uses exactly 200 unique card IDs (card_001 through card_200). See the JSON for the definitive deduplicated list.
