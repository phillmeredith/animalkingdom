# Animal Kingdom App - Educational Specification
## UK National Curriculum Integration & Engagement Design

---

## OVERVIEW

Harry's Animal Kingdom is designed as **stealth learning** — educational content disguised as card game progression. Harry collects cards, plays games, levels up his favorites, and pursues social/collection goals while systematically building competence across four core subjects.

**Core principle:** Every single question/challenge directly maps to UK National Curriculum learning objectives. Nothing is filler.

Harry thinks he's collecting cards. He's actually completing UK National Curriculum for English, Maths, Geography, and Science (Years 1-6).

---

## THE FOUR GAMES

### WORD SAFARI - English

**Years 1-2: Phonics Foundation**
- CVC words (cat, dog, bat)
- Phoneme-grapheme mapping
- Sound discrimination
- Word family chains (cat → bat → hat)
- Examples are card-specific (Beagle hunts → rabbit, etc.)

**Years 3-4: Morphology & Vocabulary**
- Root + suffix (hunt → hunting, hunted, hunter)
- Root + prefix (happy → unhappy, re-do)
- Etymology (where words come from: Latin, Greek, French, Old English)
- Antonyms and synonyms
- All questions about the selected card's species and origin

**Years 5-6: Advanced Language**
- Complex morphology (un- + comfortable + -ly)
- Subject-specific vocabulary (endangered, conservation, migration, ecosystem)
- Etymology through language history
- Nuance and connotation (curious vs nosy, strong vs aggressive)
- Word precision and register awareness

**Session structure:** 12-15 minutes, 10-12 mixed challenges (no two same type back-to-back)

**Why it works:** Every question teaches the card about itself. "Beagle hunts by following ____" (SCENT) teaches both spelling and a real fact. Intelligence stat increases show learning is building power.

---

### COIN RUSH - Mathematics

**Years 1-2: Number Sense**
- Subitising (see 5 dots, recognize 5 without counting)
- Number bonds (3 + ? = 10)
- Counting on/back (start at 7, count on 3)
- Doubling and doubles +/- 1
- Card-specific phrasing: "Beagle catches 3 rabbits, needs 10 total, how many more?"

**Years 3-4: Multiplicative Thinking & Fractions**
- Arrays (3 rows of 4 = 3 × 4)
- Division as sharing (12 rabbits split 3 ways)
- Fractions as equal parts (1/4 of day in morning, 1/4 midday, etc.)
- Place value (34 = 3 tens + 4 ones)
- Missing number problems (5 × ? = 20)

**Years 5-6: Fluency & Advanced Operations**
- Multiplication/division fluency (up to 12×12, optional timed mode)
- Fraction/decimal/percentage equivalence (1/4 = 0.25 = 25%)
- Ratio and proportion (2 predators : 5 prey ratio scaling)
- Multi-step problem solving
- Algebraic thinking (sequences, variables)

**Session structure:** 12-15 minutes, 10-15 mixed math challenges

**Why it works:** Math problems use card's actual stats. "Beagle runs at 50 km/h. Add 5 km/h. New speed?" becomes Beagle's Speed stat increases visibly (50 → 51). Different cards create different difficulty profiles (high Speed card = fast-paced problems).

---

### HABITAT BUILDER - Science

**Years 1-2: Basic Needs & Habitats**
- 5-day survival simulation in forest
- Daily decisions: Hunt, drink water, find shelter, or explore
- Consequences: Stamina depletes, food increases, condition changes
- Simple food chains (grass → rabbit → beagle)
- Basic adaptation (thick fur for winter)
- Card-specific: Beagle hunts rabbits (not berries), likes forests (not deserts)

**Years 3-4: Adaptation & Food Webs**
- Biome selection (forest, savanna, ocean, desert)
- Complex food webs (not just chains)
- Predator-prey relationships (wolves compete with beagle)
- Life cycles (beagle: puppy → adult → senior)
- Adaptation to different environments

**Years 5-6: Ecosystems & Natural Selection**
- Build ecosystems from scratch (balance producers/consumers/decomposers)
- Energy flow and trophic levels (10% efficiency rule)
- Natural selection simulation (fast rabbits survive better over generations)
- Inheritance and genetic mixing
- Conservation and human impact on ecosystems

**Session structure:** 15-20 minutes per simulation (5-day cycles)

**Why it works:** Every decision has visible cause → effect. Wrong choices create immediate consequences (less food = lower stamina). Card condition can decrease, creating stakes without pressure. Multi-card teams teach ecosystem balance (predator-prey ratios matter).

---

### WORLD QUEST - Geography

**Years 1-2: UK Locations & Direction**
- Map of UK: Find England, Scotland, Wales, Northern Ireland
- Directional language (left, right, north, south)
- Habitats (forest, grassland, desert, ocean)
- Weather and seasons
- Card origin: "Beagle comes from England"

**Years 3-4: Map Skills & Regions**
- Compass directions (N, S, E, W, NE, NW, etc.)
- UK regions and counties
- Map keys and symbols
- Grid references
- Climate zones and biomes
- Physical geography (rivers, mountains, cities)

**Years 5-6: Global Geography & Human Impact**
- Latitude and longitude system
- Why equator is hotter (sun angle, not distance)
- Migration routes (Monarch butterfly Mexico → Canada)
- Biome distribution across globe
- Human geography (settlements, habitat fragmentation)
- Conservation challenges and strategies

**Session structure:** 12-15 minutes per session, 10-12 challenges

**Why it works:** Each card teaches about its native region. Red Panda = China, bamboo forests, 30°N latitude, altitude adaptation, endangered status. Discovering new regions unlocks new cards to collect. Map fills in as player learns (visual progress).

## CARD-SPECIFIC QUESTIONS

**Every game is personalized to the selected card.**

Example: Playing Word Safari with Beagle Dog vs Red Panda
- Beagle: "Hunts by following ____" (SCENT), "Originated in ____" (ENGLAND), "Travels in a ____" (PACK)
- Red Panda: "Lives at high ____" (ALTITUDE), "Diet is mainly ____" (BAMBOO), "Is ____" (ENDANGERED)

Example: Playing Coin Rush with Beagle vs Polar Bear
- Beagle: Questions using Speed 50, Strength 40, Stamina 60 as numbers
- Polar Bear: Same question types but with Speed 45, Strength 85, Stamina 70 (different difficulty)

This means:
- **Same game, different card = completely different learning**
- **Harry can't brute-force memorize answers** (they change per card)
- **Replaying with different cards feels fresh**
- **Each card teaches something specific about that animal**

## ENGAGEMENT ARCHITECTURE

### Why Harry Keeps Returning

**1. Card Progression (Days-Weeks)**
- Every session = XP gain
- XP bar fills visibly
- Every 20-30 sessions = level up (celebration animation)
- Every 3-4 levels = evolution (rarity increase, visual change)
- Motivation: See favorite card reach Level 10

**2. Collection (Months)**
- 150+ cards available
- Each teaches different curriculum elements
- Completion tracker: "47/150 cards (31%)"
- Motivation: Never finish (long-term engagement)

**3. Variants & Cosmetics (Sessions)**
- Holographic (rare, pure cosmetic)
- Regional variants (unlock in World Quest)
- Seasonal variants (winter, spring, etc.)
- Trophy variants (tournament winners)
- Motivation: Hunt for rare versions

**4. Achievements (Sessions-Months)**
- 30+ badges across games
- Speed Learner, Pattern Master, Biome Expert, Cartographer
- Motivation: Show mastery, shareable

**5. Social Proof (Leaderboards)**
- Weekly tournaments
- Anonymized comparisons by age
- "Top 20% this week"
- Motivation: Friendly competition

**6. Personalization (Ownership)**
- Custom names: "Scout" instead of "Beagle Dog"
- Journal notes: "Found in Lake District"
- Favorites pinned to home screen
- Motivation: Cards feel personally meaningful

**7. Natural Difficulty Curve**
- Difficulty scales to card stats (Intelligence, Speed, etc.)
- Auto-adjusts after streaks
- Different cards = different profiles
- Motivation: Always challenged, never frustrated

**8. Educational Feedback Loop (Core)**
- Correct answer = visible stat increase
- Stat increase = visible power increase
- Power = ability to tackle harder challenges
- Harder challenges = deeper understanding
- **Motivation: Learning demonstrably makes card stronger**

## LEARNING GUARDRAILS

### Protecting Educational Integrity

**No dumbed-down content:**
- Every question maps to UK Curriculum
- All questions are legitimate objectives
- No filler or busywork

**Age-appropriate scaffolding:**
- Years 1-2: Manipulatives, visual support, no time pressure
- Years 3-4: Reduced visuals, gentle increase
- Years 5-6: Abstract, complex, optional speed mode

**Multiple modalities:**
- Visual (bar models, maps, diagrams)
- Kinesthetic (drag-drop, interactive)
- Auditory (phonetic, pronunciation)
- Reading/writing (text, spelling input)

**No negative messaging:**
- No "wrong answer" (feedback: "Try again")
- No time pressure as punishment
- Leaderboards anonymized (percentiles, not rankings)
- Growth mindset: Celebrate improvement

**Optimal challenge:**
- Auto-adjust to ~80% success rate
- Too easy = bored → too hard = frustrated
- Game constantly recalibrates

## HOW GAMES CONNECT

**Cross-game synergy:** Learning in one game reinforces others.

**Word Safari vocabulary used in:**
- Habitat Builder: "ENDANGERED", "CONSERVATION", "ECOSYSTEM"
- World Quest: "MIGRATION", "HABITAT", "LATITUDE"

**Coin Rush math used in:**
- Habitat Builder: Population calculations, ratios
- World Quest: Climate percentages, migration speed (distance/time)

**Habitat Builder science used in:**
- World Quest: Adaptation explains why animals live in specific regions
- Coin Rush: Predator-prey ratios feed into problem-solving

**World Quest geography used in:**
- Habitat Builder: Climate zones explain habitat variation
- Coin Rush: Latitude coordinates become data points

### Example: Red Panda Card

**Word Safari:**
- Vocabulary: ENDANGERED, BAMBOO, CONSERVATION, HABITAT, CHINA
- Intelligence increases

**Coin Rush:**
- Conservation fund: "60% raised, goal 100%, how much more?"
- Habitat: "3/4 preserved, 1/4 lost, what % is that?"
- Proportional reasoning through real numbers

**Habitat Builder:**
- Build bamboo forest biome with limited space
- Manage altitude, cool climate, bamboo food
- Learn vulnerability: small range = susceptible to habitat loss

**World Quest:**
- Red Panda origin: China, Himalayan mountains (30°N, 1200-3400m altitude)
- Latitude/altitude relationship: Why mountains are different
- Habitat fragmentation: Mountains fragmented by human development
- Conservation: Small population = genetic bottleneck

**Result:** Red Panda tells complete endangered species story across all games.

## THE HIDDEN CURRICULUM

This specification intentionally hides curriculum mechanics. Harry doesn't see "Year 5 Morphology Quiz." He sees "Teach Beagle about word-building" while playing a spelling game.

Every element serves dual purposes:
1. **Educational:** Builds curriculum competency
2. **Engaging:** Progresses card, unlocks variants, contributes to collection

**The curriculum is the payload. The card game is the delivery mechanism.**

Harry thinks he's collecting cards and leveling them up. He's actually systematically completing UK National Curriculum for English, Maths, Geography, and Science (Years 1-6).

Success looks like: Harry has collected 47 cards. He's unconsciously learned all Year 1-2 content for all four subjects, most of Year 3-4, and some Year 5-6. He didn't know he was doing it.

---

## CORE DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

**Stealth Learning through Intrinsic Motivation**

Harry is motivated by:
- Card progression (XP, leveling, evolution)
- Collection completion (47/150)
- Cosmetic rewards (variants, achievements)
- Card personalization ("Scout")
- Visible stat growth (50 → 51 Speed)

Harry is NOT motivated by:
- Test scores or grades
- Explicit lesson completion
- Achievement badges for "learning"
- Pressure or time limits (optional)

**The learning happens because:**
1. Questions are specific to card (can't brute-force memorize)
2. Correct answers directly increase card power
3. Harder challenges require deeper understanding
4. Different cards = different learning paths (can't skip)
5. Progression is visible and rewarding

**The card game is intrinsically motivating. The curriculum is invisible payload.**

This is how you teach a 6-12 year old without them realizing they're learning.
